Roy L. Clay Sr.

Roy Clay Sr. (born 1929) is an American computer scientist and inventor. He was a founding member of the computer division at Hewlett-Packard, where he led the team that created the HP 2116A. After leaving HP he established his own consulting company (Roy L Clay and Associates). His consulting company worked closely with Tom Perkins (Kleiner Perkins) and contributed to formation of Silicon Valley as we know it today.


Accomplishments and Achievements
1. First African American to graduate from St. Louis University.
2. First black council member for Palo Alto.
3. Founded ROD-L Electronics that kept early computers from catching fire.
4. Grew up in Kinloch, MO
